# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Primary Segments

### 1. Active Adults (30â€“55)
- **Who:** Working professionals and recreational athletes
- **Need:** Sports injuries, overuse conditions (tendinitis, torn meniscus), joint pain affecting daily life
- **Motivation:** Get back to work and activity quickly with minimal downtime
- **How they find us:** Google search, GP/physio referral, word of mouth
- **Decision factors:** Convenience, speed of appointment, surgeon credentials

### 2. Older Adults (55+)
- **Who:** Retirees and seniors experiencing degenerative conditions
- **Need:** Arthritis management, joint replacement, mobility preservation
- **Motivation:** Maintain independence, reduce chronic pain, improve quality of life
- **How they find us:** GP referral, family recommendation, insurance directory
- **Decision factors:** Trust, bedside manner, proximity, insurance acceptance

### 3. Acute Injury Patients (All Ages)
- **Who:** Anyone with a sudden fracture, dislocation, or traumatic injury
- **Need:** Urgent orthopaedic assessment and treatment
- **Motivation:** Immediate pain relief and proper healing
- **How they find us:** Emergency department referral, urgent care referral, direct walk-in
- **Decision factors:** Availability, proximity, after-hours access

### 4. Youth Athletes & Parents
- **Who:** School-age and collegiate athletes (and their parents)
- **Need:** Sports injury treatment, growth plate injury management, return-to-play clearance
- **Motivation:** Safe recovery, not jeopardising long-term development
- **How they find us:** Coach/trainer referral, school sports programme, parent networks
- **Decision factors:** Sports medicine expertise, family-friendly environment

## Geographic Focus

- Primary: 15-mile radius around the practice location
- Secondary: Regional referrals for sub-specialty procedures

## Anti-Personas (Not a Fit)

- Patients seeking only chiropractic or alternative medicine
- Cases requiring oncological orthopaedics (refer to academic centres)
- Patients seeking cosmetic-only procedures unrelated to musculoskeletal function
